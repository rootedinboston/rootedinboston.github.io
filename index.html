About Rooted In Boston

>>>>>>> master

Rooted in Boston is a series of virtual events from April to June that showcases the rich culture and diversity Boston has to offer, with an eye towards justice — a nod to Angela Davis’ thoughts on diversity with justice.


The theme Rooted in Boston can be interpreted two ways:

  • Based in, developed from, or influenced by Boston
  • Established or grounded, and in Boston

The series’ goal is to bring together communities to promote togetherness in a time of isolation and create and continue conversations for change.


Why this? Why now?

Diversity is core to what makes cities great. In Boston, 20-35 year olds make up more than a third of the City's population. Against the backdrop of 2020 with an unprecedented pandemic, use of divisive rhetoric, and national movement against racial injustice, there’s a need now to unite Boston and reimagine civic engagement and ways to meaningfully connect with each other.


What is SPARK Boston?

SPARK Boston is a leadership and civic engagement council made up of 20-35 year olds, charged with spearheading the City's efforts to engage Boston's young adult population.

Tech Equity
DATE/TIME TBD

Boston is an extraordinary place for entrepreneurship, from having access to a top-notch talent pool to being ranked the second best ecosystem for female founders.


As technology increasingly reshapes and directs our world, it's important to think critically about fairness and equity - from who's behind the code/idea/product to who benefits from it. Some questions to explore include: How can a tech-driven economy become more inclusive, whether or not you work in tech? How can tech play a role in giving everyone access to economic opportunity? How can young people (age 20-35 is SPARK Boston's target audience) play a role in tech equity?
